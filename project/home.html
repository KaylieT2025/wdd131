What is a Shark?

Sharks are a subclass of fish called elasmobranchii. This particular subclass has a skeleton made of cartilage not bone, unlike human skeletons. Sharks have 5-7 gills on each side of their face unlike their fish cousins that typically only have 1. Like other fish, sharks use the gills to filter the water for oxygen. Each shark has rows of razor-sharp teeth that fall and regrow routinely. Every species of shark has diffrent shapes and sizes of teeth.

What is a Shark?

Sharks are a subclass of fish called elasmobranchii. This particular subclass has a skeleton made of cartilage not bone, unlike human skeletons. Sharks have 5-7 gills on each side of their face unlike their fish cousins that typically only have 1. Like other fish, sharks use the gills to filter the water for oxygen. Each shark has rows of razor-sharp teeth that fall and regrow routinely. Every species of shark has different shapes and sizes of teeth.

How is a Shark Classified?

A shark is classified Chondrichthyes. Which just refers to fish with cartilaginous skeletons. "Chondrichthyes consists of two groups, Holocephali and Elasmobranchii, and within Elasmobranchii are eight (8) orders of sharks. Each order tells us about the biology, physical characteristics and behavior of sharks in that order."

What do I do if I see a Shark?

If you encounter to a shark when swimming do not panic. Panicking and thrashing around will attract and agitate the shark. Sharks are attracted to movements and vibrations in the water, when you thrash you are creating vibration that the shark will be drawn to. The best thing to do is to stay calm and move slowly. Do not turn your back to the shark as you slowly move to the beach. If you do get bitten by a shark, it is best to fight it. Sharks are actually lazy and want the easiest meal possible. If you fight back and make yourself difficult, such as punching, kicking, or poking it's eyes, the shark will likely give up. Get medical attention as soon as possible if you are bitten.

  • U.S. Swimming Masters
